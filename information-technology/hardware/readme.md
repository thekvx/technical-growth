# 🖥️ Hardware

Understanding computer hardware provides the foundation for building, maintaining, and troubleshooting modern computer systems. From individual components to complete workstations, hardware knowledge is an important part of supporting users and the technology they rely on.

This section documents the hardware concepts I've studied, the practical skills I've developed, and the hands-on experience I've gained through certification studies, labs, and continued practice. Everything included here reflects my personal learning journey as I continue strengthening my Information Technology foundation.

---

# 📚 Areas of Study

### 🖥️ Computer Components

Learning how major hardware components work together, including processors, memory, storage devices, motherboards, power supplies, and expansion cards.

---

### 💾 Storage & Memory

Building an understanding of storage technologies, memory, performance considerations, and how these components impact overall system functionality.

---

### 🖨️ Peripherals & Devices

Learning how computers interact with monitors, printers, keyboards, mice, mobile devices, and other common peripherals used in both home and business environments.

---

### 🔧 Installation & Maintenance

Developing practical knowledge of hardware installation, upgrades, preventative maintenance, and component replacement through continued study and hands-on practice.

---

### 🛠️ Hardware Troubleshooting

Practicing structured troubleshooting techniques to diagnose and resolve common hardware-related issues while building confidence through practical experience.

---

# 💡 Learning Philosophy

Understanding hardware helps me better understand the systems I'm supporting and securing. While hardware may not be the primary focus of my long-term career, building a strong foundation in how computer systems function makes me a more effective IT and cybersecurity professional.

This section isn't intended to be a complete hardware reference. Instead, it documents the knowledge I've personally built as I continue developing my Information Technology foundation.
