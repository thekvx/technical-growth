# 🌐 Networking

Networking is one of the core foundations of Information Technology and Cybersecurity. Understanding how devices communicate, how data moves across networks, and how common network services operate provides the context needed to troubleshoot issues and better understand how systems are secured.

This section documents the networking concepts I've studied, the practical skills I've developed, and the hands-on experience I've gained through certification studies, labs, and continued practice. Everything included here reflects my personal learning journey as I continue strengthening my technical foundation.

---

# 📚 Areas of Study

### 🌍 Network Fundamentals

Building a strong understanding of networking concepts including network models, common devices, topologies, and how systems communicate.

---

### 📡 Protocols & Services

Learning the purpose and operation of common networking protocols and services such as TCP/IP, DNS, DHCP, routing, switching, and network ports.

---

### 🛠️ Network Troubleshooting

Developing structured troubleshooting skills to identify and resolve common connectivity and network-related issues using both methodology and practical tools.

---

### 🔍 Network Analysis

Learning how to observe and analyze network traffic to better understand communication between systems and to support both troubleshooting and cybersecurity investigations.

---

### 🛡️ Networking & Cybersecurity

Building an understanding of how networking supports cybersecurity through concepts such as segmentation, firewalls, VPNs, secure communication, and network monitoring.

---

# 💡 Learning Philosophy

The more I study networking, the more I realize that it connects nearly every area of Information Technology. Rather than memorizing protocols or port numbers, I focus on understanding how systems communicate and why they behave the way they do. That foundation helps me troubleshoot more effectively and supports my long-term goal of working in cybersecurity.

This section isn't intended to be a networking textbook. Instead, it documents the knowledge I've personally built as I continue developing my Information Technology foundation.
