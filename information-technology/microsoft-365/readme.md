# ☁️ Microsoft 365

Microsoft 365 is a core part of many modern business environments and is commonly used across Information Technology and Cybersecurity. Building a strong understanding of its applications, administration, and cloud services helps support users while providing the foundation for identity, collaboration, and cloud-based security.

This section documents the Microsoft 365 concepts I've studied, the practical skills I'm developing, and the hands-on experience I continue to gain through certification studies, labs, and continued practice. Everything included here reflects my personal learning journey as I continue strengthening my Information Technology foundation.

---

# 📚 Areas of Study

### 👤 User Administration

Learning how users, licenses, and organizational settings are managed within Microsoft 365 environments.

---

### 📧 Productivity & Collaboration

Building familiarity with Microsoft applications and services such as Outlook, Teams, OneDrive, and SharePoint while learning how they support communication and collaboration.

---

### ☁️ Cloud Services

Developing an understanding of Microsoft's cloud ecosystem and the role cloud technologies play in modern Information Technology environments.

---

### 🔐 Identity Fundamentals

Learning the basic concepts of identity, authentication, and access management that support secure user administration within Microsoft environments.

---

### 🛠️ Administration & Support

Developing the knowledge needed to troubleshoot common Microsoft 365 issues, support users, and understand the administrative tools used in business environments.

---

# 💡 Learning Philosophy

Microsoft 365 is much more than a collection of productivity applications. As organizations continue moving services to the cloud, understanding how users, identities, and cloud services work together has become an important part of both Information Technology and Cybersecurity.

This section isn't intended to be a complete reference for Microsoft 365. Instead, it documents the knowledge I'm personally building as I continue developing my Information Technology foundation.
