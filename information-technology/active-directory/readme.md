# 👥 Active Directory

Active Directory is one of the core technologies used to manage users, computers, and resources within Windows-based business environments. Understanding how identities, permissions, and authentication work provides an important foundation for both Information Technology and Cybersecurity.

This section documents the Active Directory concepts I've studied, the practical skills I'm developing, and the hands-on experience I'm gaining through home labs, certification studies, and continued practice. Everything included here reflects my personal learning journey as I continue strengthening my Information Technology foundation.

---

# 📚 Areas of Study

### 👤 User & Group Management

Learning how users, groups, and organizational units are structured and managed within an Active Directory environment.

---

### 🔐 Authentication & Authorization

Building an understanding of how authentication, permissions, and access control work together to protect systems and organizational resources.

---

### 🏢 Domain Fundamentals

Learning how domains, domain controllers, forests, and organizational units work together to provide centralized administration.

---

### ⚙️ Group Policy

Developing an understanding of how Group Policy is used to manage user and computer configurations across an organization.

---

### 🛠️ Administration & Troubleshooting

Building practical knowledge through lab environments while learning common administrative tasks, troubleshooting techniques, and best practices.

---

# 💡 Learning Philosophy

Identity is at the center of modern Information Technology and Cybersecurity. The more I learn about how users, permissions, and authentication work together, the better prepared I am to support business environments and understand the security challenges that organizations face.

This section isn't intended to be a complete reference for Active Directory. Instead, it documents the knowledge I'm personally building as I continue developing my Information Technology foundation.
