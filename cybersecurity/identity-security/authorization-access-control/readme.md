# 🛡️ Authorization & Access Control

Authorization and access control determine what authenticated users, devices, and services are permitted to access within an organization. By assigning appropriate permissions and limiting unnecessary access, organizations reduce security risks while supporting users in performing their responsibilities effectively.

This section documents the authorization and access control concepts I've studied, the practical skills I'm developing, and the hands-on experience I'm gaining through certification studies, labs, and continued practice. Everything included here reflects my personal learning journey as I continue building the knowledge needed for a career in cybersecurity.

---

# 📚 Areas of Study

### 🔑 Least Privilege

Learning how limiting users and services to the minimum permissions necessary helps reduce security risks and supports a stronger security posture.

---

### 👥 Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)

Building an understanding of how permissions can be assigned based on organizational roles, making access management more consistent and easier to maintain.

---

### 🛡️ Access Control Models

Learning how different access control models help organizations protect systems, applications, and sensitive information while supporting business operations.

---

### 📊 Permission Management

Developing an understanding of how permissions are reviewed, maintained, and adjusted throughout a user's lifecycle to ensure appropriate access over time.

---

### 📚 Continuous Learning

Strengthening my understanding of authorization and access control through certification studies, hands-on labs, technical documentation, and practical exercises while continuing to expand my cybersecurity knowledge.

---

# 💡 Learning Philosophy

Authentication confirms identity, but authorization determines what that identity is allowed to do. I believe effective access control is built on thoughtful planning, consistent management, and the principle of granting only the access necessary to perform a specific role. As I continue learning, I focus on understanding how strong authorization practices contribute to a more secure and manageable environment.

This section isn't intended to be a complete reference for authorization and access control. Instead, it documents the knowledge I'm personally building as I continue working toward a career in cybersecurity.
