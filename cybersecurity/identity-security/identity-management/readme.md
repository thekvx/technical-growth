# 👤 Identity Management

Identity management focuses on creating, maintaining, and securing user identities throughout their lifecycle. By ensuring that users receive the appropriate access at the appropriate time, organizations can better protect systems, data, and resources while supporting business operations.

This section documents the identity management concepts I've studied, the practical skills I'm developing, and the hands-on experience I'm gaining through certification studies, labs, and continued practice. Everything included here reflects my personal learning journey as I continue building the knowledge needed for a career in cybersecurity.

---

# 📚 Areas of Study

### 👤 Identity Lifecycle

Learning how user identities are created, maintained, modified, and securely removed throughout their lifecycle within an organization.

---

### 📂 Account Management

Building an understanding of how user accounts, groups, and organizational roles contribute to secure identity management and effective access control.

---

### 🛡️ Identity Governance

Learning how policies, procedures, and security practices help organizations manage identities consistently while reducing unnecessary risk.

---

### 🔄 Identity Synchronization

Developing an understanding of how identities are managed across multiple systems and how centralized identity services support secure, efficient administration.

---

### 📚 Continuous Learning

Strengthening my understanding of identity management through certification studies, hands-on labs, technical documentation, and practical exercises while continuing to expand my cybersecurity knowledge.

---

# 💡 Learning Philosophy

Identity management is about more than creating user accounts. It establishes the foundation for secure access, accountability, and effective cybersecurity practices across an organization. As I continue learning, I focus on understanding how well-managed identities contribute to stronger security while supporting the needs of both users and administrators.

This section isn't intended to be a complete reference for identity management. Instead, it documents the knowledge I'm personally building as I continue working toward a career in cybersecurity.
