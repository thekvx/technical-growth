# 🔑 Authentication

Authentication is the process of verifying the identity of a user, device, or service before granting access to systems and resources. Strong authentication helps reduce unauthorized access and serves as one of the foundational security controls in modern cybersecurity.

This section documents the authentication concepts I've studied, the practical skills I'm developing, and the hands-on experience I'm gaining through certification studies, labs, and continued practice. Everything included here reflects my personal learning journey as I continue building the knowledge needed for a career in cybersecurity.

---

# 📚 Areas of Study

### 🔐 Authentication Factors

Learning how knowledge, possession, and inherence factors work together to verify identities and strengthen account security.

---

### 📱 Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Building an understanding of how multiple authentication factors significantly improve account security by reducing the risk of unauthorized access.

---

### 🔑 Password Security

Learning the importance of strong password practices, secure credential management, and modern approaches that reduce dependence on passwords alone.

---

### 🛡️ Modern Authentication

Developing an understanding of emerging authentication methods such as passwordless authentication, passkeys, biometrics, and other technologies that strengthen identity verification.

---

### 📚 Continuous Learning

Strengthening my understanding of authentication through certification studies, hands-on labs, technical documentation, and practical exercises while continuing to expand my cybersecurity knowledge.

---

# 💡 Learning Philosophy

Authentication is about establishing trust before access is granted. As authentication technologies continue to evolve, I focus on understanding the underlying principles rather than memorizing individual products or platforms. Building that foundation helps me adapt to new technologies while maintaining a strong understanding of secure identity verification.

This section isn't intended to be a complete reference for authentication. Instead, it documents the knowledge I'm personally building as I continue working toward a career in cybersecurity.
