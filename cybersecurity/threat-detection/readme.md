# 🎯 Threat Detection

Threat detection is the process of identifying suspicious or malicious activity within an environment before it becomes a larger security incident. It combines technical knowledge, observation, and analysis to recognize behaviors that may indicate unauthorized or harmful activity.

This section documents the threat detection concepts I've studied, the practical skills I'm developing, and the hands-on experience I'm gaining through certification studies, labs, and continued practice. Everything included here reflects my personal learning journey as I continue building the knowledge needed for a career in cybersecurity.

---

# 📚 Areas of Study

### 📊 Log Analysis

Learning how logs provide visibility into system activity and how they can be used to identify unusual behavior and support security investigations.

---

### 🚩 Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)

Building an understanding of common indicators that suggest malicious activity, including suspicious files, IP addresses, domains, hashes, and user behavior.

---

### 🧠 Threat Intelligence

Learning how publicly available and organizational threat intelligence can provide context for detecting and understanding emerging threats.

---

### 🔍 Security Analysis

Developing the ability to review alerts, examine available evidence, recognize suspicious patterns, and determine when additional investigation is needed.

---

### 📈 Continuous Improvement

Building stronger analytical skills by reviewing detection methods, understanding missed opportunities, and continuously improving the ability to recognize suspicious activity.

---

# 💡 Learning Philosophy

Threat detection is about understanding what "normal" looks like so that unusual behavior stands out. Rather than relying on memorization alone, I focus on understanding why an alert was generated, what evidence supports it, and how multiple pieces of information come together to tell the larger story.

This section isn't intended to be a complete reference for threat detection. Instead, it documents the knowledge I'm personally building as I continue working toward a career in cybersecurity.
