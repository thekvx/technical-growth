# 🧠 Threat Intelligence

Threat intelligence provides context that helps security professionals better understand emerging threats, attacker behavior, and indicators that may be relevant to their environment. Rather than focusing only on individual security events, threat intelligence helps connect technical findings to the broader threat landscape.

This section documents the threat intelligence concepts I've studied, the practical skills I'm developing, and the hands-on experience I'm gaining through certification studies, labs, and continued practice. Everything included here reflects my personal learning journey as I continue building the knowledge needed for a career in cybersecurity.

---

# 📚 Areas of Study

### 🌍 Threat Landscape

Learning how cybersecurity threats evolve over time and how different threat actors, attack techniques, and campaigns contribute to today's security landscape.

---

### 📖 Intelligence Sources

Building an understanding of publicly available threat intelligence, vendor research, security advisories, and other trusted sources that help provide context for emerging threats.

---

### 🎯 Indicators & Context

Learning how threat intelligence supports investigations by adding context to indicators of compromise, security alerts, and suspicious activity.

---

### 🔗 Threat Correlation

Developing the ability to connect threat intelligence with available evidence to better understand potential risks and support informed decision-making.

---

### 📚 Continuous Learning

Strengthening my understanding of threat intelligence through certification studies, technical articles, hands-on labs, and practical exercises while continuing to expand my cybersecurity knowledge.

---

# 💡 Learning Philosophy

Threat intelligence isn't about memorizing threat actors or attack techniques. It's about understanding how available information provides context, supports investigations, and helps explain why security events matter. As I continue learning, I focus on building the habit of asking questions, researching available information, and using evidence to develop a more complete understanding of potential threats.

This section isn't intended to be a complete reference for threat intelligence. Instead, it documents the knowledge I'm personally building as I continue working toward a career in cybersecurity.
