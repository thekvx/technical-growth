# 🚩 Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)

Indicators of Compromise (IOCs) are pieces of information that may suggest malicious activity has occurred within an environment. They help security professionals recognize suspicious behavior, support investigations, and identify potential security incidents more efficiently.

This section documents the IOC concepts I've studied, the practical skills I'm developing, and the hands-on experience I'm gaining through certification studies, labs, and continued practice. Everything included here reflects my personal learning journey as I continue building the knowledge needed for a career in cybersecurity.

---

# 📚 Areas of Study

### 🌐 Network Indicators

Learning how IP addresses, domains, URLs, and other network-based indicators can provide valuable clues during security investigations.

---

### 📄 File Indicators

Building an understanding of file hashes, filenames, digital signatures, and other artifacts that may help identify suspicious or malicious files.

---

### 👤 Behavioral Indicators

Learning how unusual user activity, unexpected system behavior, and abnormal authentication events may indicate malicious activity or compromised accounts.

---

### 🔗 IOC Correlation

Developing the ability to evaluate multiple indicators together to better understand security events rather than relying on a single piece of evidence.

---

### 📚 Continuous Learning

Strengthening my understanding of Indicators of Compromise through certification studies, hands-on labs, technical documentation, and practical exercises while continuing to expand my investigative skills.

---

# 💡 Learning Philosophy

An individual indicator rarely tells the complete story. I believe effective threat detection comes from examining multiple pieces of evidence, understanding their context, and recognizing how they relate to one another. As I continue learning, I focus on developing the analytical mindset needed to evaluate indicators thoughtfully rather than making assumptions based on isolated observations.

This section isn't intended to be a complete reference for Indicators of Compromise. Instead, it documents the knowledge I'm personally building as I continue working toward a career in cybersecurity.
