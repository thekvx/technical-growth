# 🚨 Alert Triage

Alert triage is the process of reviewing and evaluating security alerts to determine their priority, potential impact, and whether additional investigation is necessary. It helps Security Operations teams focus their attention on the events that present the greatest risk while reducing unnecessary time spent on false positives.

This section documents the alert triage concepts I've studied, the practical skills I'm developing, and the hands-on experience I'm gaining through certification studies, labs, and continued practice. Everything included here reflects my personal learning journey as I continue building the knowledge needed for a career in cybersecurity.

---

# 📚 Areas of Study

### 🚦 Alert Prioritization

Learning how alerts are categorized and prioritized based on severity, potential impact, and the likelihood that malicious activity has occurred.

---

### 🔍 Initial Assessment

Building an understanding of how available information, context, and supporting evidence help determine whether an alert requires further investigation.

---

### ⚠️ False Positives

Learning how expected system behavior, misconfigurations, or normal user activity can generate alerts that do not represent actual security incidents.

---

### 📄 Documentation & Escalation

Developing the habit of documenting observations, recording supporting evidence, and understanding when an alert should be escalated for additional analysis.

---

### 📚 Continuous Learning

Strengthening my analytical thinking through hands-on labs, technical documentation, and practical exercises while improving my ability to evaluate security events with confidence.

---

# 💡 Learning Philosophy

Effective alert triage isn't about making quick decisions. It's about gathering information, understanding context, and making thoughtful decisions based on the available evidence. As I continue learning, I focus on developing a structured approach that emphasizes accuracy, consistency, and continuous improvement.

This section isn't intended to be a complete reference for alert triage. Instead, it documents the knowledge I'm personally building as I continue working toward a career in cybersecurity.
