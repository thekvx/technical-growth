# 🔎 Investigation

Investigation is a critical phase of incident response. Once a potential security incident has been identified, investigators gather available information, examine evidence, and develop an understanding of what occurred to support an effective response and recovery effort.

This section documents the investigation concepts I've studied, the practical skills I'm developing, and the hands-on experience I'm gaining through certification studies, labs, and continued practice. Everything included here reflects my personal learning journey as I continue building the knowledge needed for a career in cybersecurity.

---

# 📚 Areas of Study

### 📋 Information Gathering

Learning how available logs, alerts, system activity, and other sources of information contribute to understanding a security incident.

---

### 🧩 Evidence Evaluation

Building an understanding of how available evidence is reviewed objectively while separating confirmed observations from assumptions.

---

### 🔗 Timeline Development

Learning how events can be organized into a timeline to better understand the sequence of actions that occurred during a security incident.

---

### 📄 Findings & Documentation

Developing the habit of documenting investigative findings, supporting evidence, and observations in a clear, organized, and repeatable manner.

---

### 📚 Continuous Learning

Strengthening my investigative skills through certification studies, hands-on labs, technical documentation, and practical exercises while continuing to improve my analytical thinking.

---

# 💡 Learning Philosophy

Effective investigations are built on curiosity, evidence, and a structured approach to problem solving. I believe that understanding what happened requires gathering information carefully, asking thoughtful questions, and allowing the available evidence to guide the investigation. As I continue learning, I focus on developing investigative habits that emphasize accuracy, organization, and continuous improvement.

This section isn't intended to be a complete reference for incident investigations. Instead, it documents the knowledge I'm personally building as I continue working toward a career in cybersecurity.
