# 🛡️ Containment & Recovery

Containment and recovery are essential phases of incident response that focus on limiting the impact of security incidents, restoring affected systems, and helping organizations return to normal operations safely. These activities reduce ongoing risk while supporting long-term resilience against future incidents.

This section documents the containment and recovery concepts I've studied, the practical skills I'm developing, and the hands-on experience I'm gaining through certification studies, labs, and continued practice. Everything included here reflects my personal learning journey as I continue building the knowledge needed for a career in cybersecurity.

---

# 📚 Areas of Study

### 🛑 Incident Containment

Learning how organizations limit the spread and impact of security incidents while protecting critical systems and preserving important evidence whenever possible.

---

### 🔧 Recovery Planning

Building an understanding of how organizations restore systems, verify normal operations, and reduce the likelihood of recurring incidents.

---

### ⚙️ System Restoration

Learning how secure configurations, validation, and careful testing contribute to safely returning affected systems to production.

---

### 📊 Risk Reduction

Developing an understanding of how security improvements, updated procedures, and strengthened controls help reduce the impact of future security incidents.

---

### 📚 Continuous Learning

Strengthening my understanding of containment and recovery through certification studies, hands-on labs, technical documentation, and practical exercises while continuing to expand my cybersecurity knowledge.

---

# 💡 Learning Philosophy

Responding to a security incident is only part of the process. Restoring normal operations safely and learning from the experience are equally important. As I continue learning, I focus on understanding how thoughtful planning, structured response procedures, and continuous improvement help organizations recover more effectively while strengthening their overall security posture.

This section isn't intended to be a complete reference for containment and recovery. Instead, it documents the knowledge I'm personally building as I continue working toward a career in cybersecurity.
