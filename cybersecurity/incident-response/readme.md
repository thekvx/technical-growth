# 🚨 Incident Response

Incident Response focuses on how organizations prepare for, respond to, and recover from security incidents. A structured response helps reduce the impact of an incident while supporting business continuity and improving future security practices.

This section documents the incident response concepts I've studied, the practical skills I'm developing, and the hands-on experience I'm gaining through certification studies, labs, and continued practice. Everything included here reflects my personal learning journey as I continue building the knowledge needed for a career in cybersecurity.

---

# 📚 Areas of Study

### 📋 Incident Response Lifecycle

Learning the phases of incident response, including preparation, identification, containment, eradication, recovery, and lessons learned.

---

### 🔎 Investigation

Building an understanding of how security events are analyzed, evidence is reviewed, and findings are documented to better understand what occurred.

---

### 🤝 Communication & Coordination

Learning how effective communication, documentation, and teamwork support successful incident response across both technical and non-technical teams.

---

### 🛡️ Containment & Recovery

Developing an understanding of the strategies used to limit the impact of security incidents, restore normal operations, and reduce future risk.

---

### 📈 Continuous Improvement

Recognizing that every incident provides an opportunity to strengthen processes, improve defensive strategies, and expand technical knowledge.

---

# 💡 Learning Philosophy

Incident Response is about more than solving technical problems. It requires preparation, communication, critical thinking, and a structured approach to decision-making under pressure. I enjoy learning how organizations respond to incidents while continuously improving the processes that help protect their systems and users.

This section isn't intended to be a complete reference for Incident Response. Instead, it documents the knowledge I'm personally building as I continue working toward a career in cybersecurity.
